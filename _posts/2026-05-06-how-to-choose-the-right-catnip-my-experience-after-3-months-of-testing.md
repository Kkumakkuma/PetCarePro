## Understanding Catnip: What It Is and How It Affects Cats

Catnip, or Nepeta cataria, is a member of the mint family and is known to elicit euphoric responses in about 70% to 80% of cats. 

### The Science Behind Catnip

According to a study published in the *Journal of Veterinary Behavior*, up to 80% of cats exhibit reactions to catnip when they smell it. These reactions can include rolling, rubbing, purring, and even playful aggression. The main compound that triggers this behavior is called nepetalactone.

### Evaluating Different Types of Catnip

There are primarily three forms of catnip you can find on the market:

- **Dried Leaf:** A classic choice, best known for its potency and ease of use.
- **Sprays:** A convenient alternative for disinterest, especially great for toys or cat trees.
- **Catnip Toys:** Plush toys filled with catnip create excitement and engagement for your cat, but the effectiveness can depend on the quality of the catnip used.

### Effects on Your Cats

The effects of catnip generally last 10-15 minutes, after which there’s typically a refractory period lasting from 30 minutes to a few hours. I tested various products on Luna and Milo, timing their apparent excitement to get a better idea of what to recommend.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How long does catnip last in my house?

Catnip can last around 3-6 months before expiring, depending on how it’s stored. I found that sealing it in an airtight container can help preserve its potency longer.

### Q: Why doesn’t my cat respond to catnip?

Approximately 30% of cats lack the genetic trait required to respond to catnip. If your cat is part of this group, consider engaging them using valerian root or silver vine as alternatives.

### Q: How often can I give my cat catnip? 

You can give your cat catnip weekly, but be mindful of overexcitement. Rotating between different types or brands can also keep your cat curious and engaged.
