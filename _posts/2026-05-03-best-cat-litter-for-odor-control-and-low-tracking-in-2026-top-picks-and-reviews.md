## The Basics of Cat Litter Types

Understanding the different types of cat litter can help you narrow down your options based on your specific needs. Here’s a quick rundown of the most common types you might consider for your feline companion.

### Clumping Clay Litter

Clumping clay litter is made from sodium bentonite, a natural clay material. This type is designed to form hard clumps when it comes into contact with moisture, making it easy to scoop out waste. It typically offers excellent odor control, but you may need to deal with tracking, as the granules are small and lightweight.

### Non-Clumping Clay Litter

While non-clumping clay litter absorbs moisture, it doesn’t form clumps. This can lead to a less efficient cleaning experience, and the litter may need to be changed more frequently. Despite this, some owners prefer it for its cost-effectiveness.

### Biodegradable Litter

This category includes materials such as recycled paper, wood, and corn. These litters are friendlier to the environment but can vary in efficiency for odor control. Some biodegradable options, like those made from corn, can actually clump, giving them the best of both worlds.

### Silica Gel Litter

Silica gel litter is made from tiny silica crystals that absorb moisture but don’t clump. They are highly efficient in controlling odors, but some users find the cleaning process to be less appealing compared to clumping options.

### Crystal Litter

Crystal litter mixes silica gel and other materials designed to absorb moisture. It’s effective at controlling odor and generally low in tracking, but it can be pricier than other options.
